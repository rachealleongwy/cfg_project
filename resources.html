Why learn to code?


Programming jobs overall are growing 12% faster than the market average as companies in the tech sector race to innovate and improve operational efficiency. There are equally as many openings in industries outside of technology such as finance, manufacturing and healthcare.

Coding skills are in high demand, yet statistics show that girls are being left behind. Gender differences in STEM education participation at the expense of girls are visible in higher education, where women represent only 35% of all students enrolled in STEM fields, especially ICT. In reality, coding is the language of the future and it should equally accessible to anyone with the desire to learn, regardless of gender or background.

Code First: Girls


Code First: Girls is a multi-award winning social enterprise that aims to increase diversity in tech through training, building communities and corporate activities.

They offer free in-person part-time coding courses for female/non binary identifying young individuals across the UK and Ireland. Full length community courses last 8 weeks.

Codecademy


Codecademy is an online interactive platform that offers free coding classes in 12 different programming languages including Python, Java, JavaScript, Ruby, SQL, and Sass, as well as markup languages HTML and CSS. The platform also provides courses for learning command line and Git.

The site offers a paid "pro" option that gives users access to a personalized learning plan, quizzes, realistic projects, and live help from advisors.

HackerRank


HackerRank is a technology company that focuses on competitive programming challenges for both consumers and businesses, where developers compete by trying to program according to provided specifications.

Programmers are then ranked globally on the HackerRank leaderboard and earn badges based on their accomplishments to drive competition among users.

General Assembly


General Assembly offers programs in web development, data science and analysis, user experience design, digital marketing, product management and more. Students can choose from a range of formats and modalities to help them best achieve their goals, including full-time, part-time, and short-form options — on campus, and online.

GA Dash is General Assembly’s free online learning platform which teaches the basics of web design through project-based tutorials.
